# The Art of War (孫子兵法) — Key Passages for Strategic Advice

## Chapter 1: Laying Plans (始計)
*"兵者，國之大事，死生之地，存亡之道，不可不察也。"*
"War is a matter of vital importance to the State; the province of life or death; the road to survival or ruin. It is mandatory that it be thoroughly studied."

*"兵者，詭道也。"*
"All warfare is based on deception."

**Five fundamental factors:** The Moral Law (道), Heaven (天, weather/timing), Earth (地, terrain), The Commander (將), Method and Discipline (法).

*"故經之以五事，校之以計，而索其情：一曰道，二曰天，三曰地，四曰將，五曰法。"*
"Therefore, in your deliberations, when seeking to determine the military conditions, let them be made the basis of a comparison, in this wise: Which of the two sovereigns is imbued with the Moral Law? Which of the two generals has most ability? With whom lie the advantages derived from Heaven and Earth? On which side is discipline most rigorously enforced?"

## Chapter 2: Waging War (作戰)
*"故兵聞拙速，未睹巧之久也。"*
"In war, I have heard of reckless haste, but I have never seen cleverness associated with long delays."

*"故不盡知用兵之害者，則不能盡知用兵之利也。"*
"He who does not know the evils of war cannot appreciate its benefits."

**Key insight for supply:** A prolonged campaign drains the treasury and exhausts the army. Win quickly or face ruin through attrition.

## Chapter 3: Attack by Stratagem (謀攻)
*"故上兵伐謀，其次伐交，其次伐兵，其下攻城。"*
"The supreme art of war is to subdue the enemy without fighting."

*"知彼知己，百戰不殆；不知彼而知己，一勝一負；不知彼，不知己，每戰必殆。"*
"If you know the enemy and know yourself, you need not fear the result of a hundred battles. If you know yourself but not the enemy, for every victory gained you will also suffer a defeat. If you know neither the enemy nor yourself, you will succumb in every battle."

**Application in Alkaid:** Use scouts (斥候) to reveal enemy positions. Knowledge of enemy composition determines optimal counter-units.

## Chapter 4: Tactical Dispositions (軍形)
*"昔之善戰者，先為不可勝，以待敵之可勝。"*
"The good fighters of old first put themselves beyond the possibility of defeat, and then waited for an opportunity of defeating the enemy."

*"不可勝在己，可勝在敵。"*
"To secure ourselves against defeat lies in our own hands, but the opportunity of defeating the enemy is provided by the enemy himself."

**Application:** Establish strong defensive positions first (hold terrain bonuses), then strike when the enemy overextends.

## Chapter 5: Energy (兵勢)
*"凡戰者，以正合，以奇勝。"*
"In battle, use the normal force to engage; use the extraordinary to win."

*"故善出奇者，無窮如天地，不竭如江河。"*
"Whoever is first in the field and awaits the coming of the enemy, will be fresh for the fight; whoever is second in the field and has to hasten to battle, will arrive exhausted."

**Application:** Pin the enemy with your main force (正, zhèng — halberdiers/swordsmen), then flank with cavalry or hidden units (奇, qí).

## Chapter 6: Weak Points and Strong (虛實)
*"故善戰者，致人而不致於人。"*
"Therefore the clever combatant imposes his will on the enemy, but does not allow the enemy's will to be imposed on him."

*"兵之形，避實而擊虛。"*
"Military tactics are like water; for water in its natural course runs away from high places and hastens downwards. So in war, avoid what is strong and strike at what is weak."

**Application:** Don't attack fortified positions head-on. Find the weak flank, cut supply lines, exploit terrain gaps.

## Chapter 7: Maneuvering (軍爭)
*"故其疾如風，其徐如林，侵掠如火，不動如山。"*
"Let your rapidity be that of the wind, your compactness that of the forest, in raiding and plundering be like fire, in stillness like a mountain."

*"以迂為直，以患為利。"*
"By discovering the enemy's dispositions and remaining invisible ourselves, we can keep our forces concentrated while the enemy's must be divided."

**Application:** Force march (疾如風) only when the advantage is decisive. The fatigue cost (+3/tick) mirrors Sun Tzu's warning about forced marches.

## Chapter 9: The Army on the March (行軍)
*"凡處軍相敵：絕山依谷，視生處高，戰隆無登。"*
"When you come to a hill or a bank, occupy the sunny side, with the slope on your right rear. Thus you will at once act for the benefit of your soldiers and utilize the natural advantages of the ground."

**Application:** Defend from hills (+40% defense bonus) and mountains (+50%). Never attack uphill if avoidable.

## Chapter 10: Terrain (地形)
*"夫地形者，兵之助也。"*
"Terrain is the soldier's best ally."

Six types of terrain: Accessible, Entangling, Temporizing, Narrow passes, Precipitous heights, Distant positions.

**Application in Alkaid:** Plains favor cavalry, forests favor swordsmen, hills favor defenders, marshes drain all units equally. Choose your battlefield.

## Chapter 11: The Nine Situations (九地)
*"投之亡地然後存，陷之死地然後生。"*
"Throw your soldiers into positions whence there is no escape, and they will prefer death to flight."

**Application:** Encircled units (no retreat path) lose morale rapidly but may fight with desperate fury. The encirclement factor in surrender pressure (15% weight) reflects this.

## Chapter 12: Attack by Fire (火攻)
*"凡火攻，必因五火之變而應之。"*
"There are five ways of attacking with fire."

**Application:** Fire ships (火船) are devastating with wind (+50% damage). The Battle of Red Cliffs was won by fire and wind — these same mechanics exist in Alkaid.

## Chapter 13: The Use of Spies (用間)
*"故明君賢將，所以動而勝人，成功出於衆者，先知也。"*
"What enables the wise sovereign and the good general to strike and conquer, and achieve things beyond the reach of ordinary men, is foreknowledge."

**Application:** Scouts (斥候) provide fog-of-war vision at 3x normal range. Information superiority enables ambushes and prevents them.
