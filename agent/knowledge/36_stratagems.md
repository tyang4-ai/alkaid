# The Thirty-Six Stratagems (三十六計)

Ancient Chinese military strategies organized into six categories.

## Winning Stratagems (勝戰計)

### 1. Cross the sea without the emperor's knowledge (瞞天過海)
Hide your true intentions behind routine actions. In Alkaid: move your main force openly while sending cavalry on a hidden flanking route through forests.

### 2. Besiege Wei to rescue Zhao (圍魏救趙)
Attack the enemy's weakness to relieve pressure elsewhere. In Alkaid: if the enemy is pushing your center, send cavalry to raid their supply depot — they'll have to divert forces.

### 3. Kill with a borrowed knife (借刀殺人)
Use a third party's strength to eliminate your enemy. In Alkaid: lure enemy cavalry into your halberdiers' anti-cavalry zone by positioning archers as bait.

### 4. Wait at leisure while the enemy labors (以逸待勞)
Let the enemy exhaust themselves. In Alkaid: hold defensive positions on hills while the enemy force-marches to attack. Their fatigue (+3/tick) makes them 35-50% weaker on arrival.

### 5. Loot a burning house (趁火打劫)
Strike when the enemy is in crisis. In Alkaid: when enemy morale cascades (30%+ routing), commit your reserves for maximum damage against fleeing troops (+50% damage vs routing).

### 6. Make a sound in the east, then strike in the west (聲東擊西)
Create a diversion to draw attention away from the real attack. In Alkaid: demonstrate with infantry on one flank while your main cavalry force charges the other side.

## Enemy-Dealing Stratagems (敵戰計)

### 7. Create something from nothing (無中生有)
Deceive the enemy with false signals. In Alkaid: use scouts' stealth to create the illusion of a larger force by appearing at multiple locations.

### 8. Openly repair the gallery roads, but sneak through the passage of Chencang (暗渡陳倉)
Attack via an unexpected route while making an obvious feint. In Alkaid: send a visible force along roads while your real attack comes through forest terrain with Dao Swordsmen (no forest penalty).

### 9. Watch the fires burning across the river (隔岸觀火)
Let your enemies destroy each other. Wait for the right moment to intervene.

### 10. Hide a knife behind a smile (笑裡藏刀)
Appear friendly or passive, then strike suddenly. In Alkaid: hold position as if defending, then unleash a devastating charge when the enemy commits to attack.

### 11. Sacrifice the plum tree to preserve the peach tree (李代桃僵)
Sacrifice something minor to gain a major advantage. In Alkaid: sacrifice a small scout squad to reveal the enemy's full position, enabling a decisive ambush.

### 12. Take the opportunity to pilfer a goat (順手牽羊)
Exploit small opportunities as they arise. In Alkaid: when enemy units are out of position, pick them off individually rather than engaging the full army.

## Attacking Stratagems (攻戰計)

### 13. Stomp the grass to scare the snake (打草驚蛇)
Probe the enemy to reveal their disposition. In Alkaid: send scouts forward to trigger enemy responses, revealing their army composition and positions.

### 14. Borrow a corpse to resurrect the soul (借屍還魂)
Revive something old in a new context. In Alkaid: rally routed units (if morale recovers above threshold + 15) to rejoin the fight.

### 15. Entice the tiger to leave its mountain lair (調虎離山)
Lure the enemy away from their advantageous position. In Alkaid: use horse archers to harass defenders on hills, drawing them into the open where cavalry dominates.

### 16. In order to capture, one must let loose (欲擒故縱)
Allow the enemy room to retreat, then pursue with advantage. In Alkaid: don't fully encircle — leave one retreat path open. Fleeing enemies take +50% damage from pursuing cavalry.

### 17. Tossing out a brick to get a jade gem (拋磚引玉)
Offer something of small value to gain something of great value. In Alkaid: sacrifice cheap scouts (60 gold) to bait enemy cavalry into halberdier formations (1.5x anti-cavalry damage).

### 18. Defeat the enemy by capturing their chief (擒賊擒王)
Target the enemy's leadership. In Alkaid: kill the enemy general to inflict -30 morale army-wide, halve command speed, and cause 15% order misinterpretation. This often triggers total army collapse.

## Chaos Stratagems (混戰計)

### 19. Remove the firewood from under the pot (釜底抽薪)
Remove the source of the enemy's strength. In Alkaid: cut supply lines with light cavalry. Without food, the enemy army starves: -40% combat, -30% movement, desertion of 1.5 soldiers/tick.

### 20. Disturb the water and catch a fish (混水摸魚)
Create confusion to gain advantage. In Alkaid: attack at night with fog — visibility drops to 15%, ambush damage doubles, and all units suffer -10 morale from fear.

### 21. Slough off the cicada's golden shell (金蟬脫殼)
Retreat while maintaining the appearance of presence. In Alkaid: pull back your main force while leaving a screening force to delay the enemy.

### 22. Shut the door to catch the thief (關門捉賊)
Encircle the enemy to prevent escape. In Alkaid: surround the enemy to maximize encirclement factor (0-100 in surrender pressure calculation, 15% weight). With no retreat paths, surrender comes faster.

### 23. Befriend a distant state and strike a nearby one (遠交近攻)
Campaign strategy. In Alkaid: choose which adjacent territories to attack based on strategic value, not proximity alone.

### 24. Obtain safe passage to conquer the State of Guo (假途伐虢)
Use an indirect approach. In Alkaid: attack a weaker territory first to gain resources and positioning for the real objective.

## Proximity Stratagems (並戰計)

### 25. Replace the beams with rotten timbers (偷梁換柱)
Undermine the enemy's structure. In Alkaid: destroy the enemy supply depot to eliminate their food reserves entirely.

### 26. Point at the mulberry tree and curse the locust tree (指桑罵槐)
Discipline through indirect example.

### 27. Pretend to be a pig in order to eat the tiger (扮豬吃虎)
Appear weak to encourage enemy aggression, then counter-attack at full strength.

### 28. Remove the ladder when the enemy has ascended to the roof (上屋抽梯)
Cut off the enemy's retreat after they've committed. In Alkaid: let the enemy advance deep into your territory, then cut behind them with cavalry to sever their supply line and retreat path.

### 29. Deck the tree with false blossoms (樹上開花)
Enhance your apparent strength. In Alkaid: spread your units wide to appear as a larger force.

### 30. Make the host and the guest exchange roles (反客為主)
Turn the enemy's attack into your advantage. In Alkaid: a defensive position on hills forces the attacker to become the weakened party.

## Desperate Stratagems (敗戰計)

### 31. The beauty trap (美人計)
Use attraction to distract.

### 32. The empty fort strategy (空城計)
When critically weak, display no defense to make the enemy suspect a trap. Zhuge Liang's famous bluff against Sima Yi.

### 33. Let the enemy's own spy sow discord in the enemy camp (反間計)
Turn the enemy's intelligence against them.

### 34. Inflict injury on oneself to win the enemy's trust (苦肉計)
Huang Gai at Red Cliffs — pretended to defect, then attacked with fire ships.

### 35. Chain stratagems (連環計)
Combine multiple stratagems. Pang Tong's chain strategy at Red Cliffs — convinced Cao Cao to chain his ships together, making them vulnerable to fire attack.

### 36. If all else fails, retreat (走為上計)
Sometimes the best strategy is to withdraw and fight another day. In Alkaid: retreat preserves your experienced units for the next battle. Losing raw recruits is cheaper than losing veterans.
